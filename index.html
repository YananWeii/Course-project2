Cyberpunk 2077

Cyberpunk 2077 is an action role-playing game developed by CD Projekt RED for PC, Xbox and PS4 on December 10, 2020. Cyberpunk 2077 takes place in the City of Night, where a change of power and physical transformation are constant themes. Players will take on the role of an ambitious mercenary: V, in pursuit of a unique implant - the key to immortality. Customise your character's prosthetic body, skills and play style and explore the all-encompassing city. The choices players make will also have an impact on the plot and the world around them.

Game Setting

In Cyberpunk 2077's overhead worldview, the United States has been torn apart by a series of wars and terrorist attacks in the late 20th century, leaving the federal government extremely vulnerable. Major multinational high-tech corporations have taken advantage of the situation and taken control of every aspect of people's lives, and the gap between the rich and the poor has become wider than ever. Peasants lost their land to takeover and annexation by large corporations, and ended up roaming the big cities in family units as paramilitary organisations; the lower and middle classes were separated from their families by mass unemployment, and ended up forming street gangs. The workplace is a dangerous and deceitful environment. The player will choose from one of these three backgrounds and begin the journey.

Introduction to the main character

The protagonist of Cyberpunk 2077 is V. V is the character that the player has to control and at the beginning of the game, three identities of V are provided and the player will choose one of them to start the game. The different identities will not only determine how the player starts the game, but will also in some way affect the protagonist's relationship with several other major characters.

V Three identities available to the player

(Vagabond): wandering the badlands, raiding scrapyards, plundering oil depots and making a living on the highways. Raised by a family of vagabonds. Honesty, integrity, and a love of freedom.

(Street Kid): Raised on a hundred families and living life on the streets. Lived among gangs, middlemen, sex couples and uncouth drug dealers. Living in the city of the night where the underdog is strong and has the law of the jungle.

(company employee): experienced a lot, flexible, profiting from other people's secrets, intelligence is the weapon in hand. There is no such thing as fair play, only success and failure.

Ghostrunner

Ghostrunner is a first-person perspective hardcore decapitation game that combines lightning-fast action with violent combat, set in a cyberpunk mega-building in a harsh environment. It tells the story of a world that has ended, and its inhabitants who are fighting to survive in it. Players will act as the most advanced blade warriors, you are always outnumbered and never outmatched. Slice and dice enemies with your monomolecular katana, dodge bullets with your super human reflexes and use a plethora of special skills to win in sparring matches.

In the earlier demo, Ghostrunner blindly listened to the 'whispers in his head' as he made his way through the bottom of Dharma Tower, ending with a meeting between the two. Now we know that the big blue head is called Architect, and it will guide you through the journey.

Also singled out is the game's villain, the Dr. Octopus-like character at the end of the trailer, the scientist Mara, known as the Keymaster. Like many power-hungry villains, Mara will do anything to take control of Dharmata, eliminating anyone who gets in her way. There are brief hints that she nearly killed the Ghostwalker, and the Builder will help you reclaim your many lost abilities and prepare you for your destined encounter with Mara. From the bottom to the top, players will take revenge against the tyrant Keymaster.

Game Setting

The tip of the iceberg in the earlier demo version of Dharma Tower, and this bottom area is simply to familiarise players with the action and pre-requisite abilities such as Sensory Boost (similar to bullet time) and grappling hook. As you climb and jump and evade the chase, you'll encounter a variety of enemies and dangers. The only things that will keep you alive are your own powerful cybernetic body and the deadly blade in your hand, which you can use to kill your enemies with a single swipe.

One of the most interesting aspects of the gameplay is that when taking damage, the player also kills in one hit. The combat is therefore somewhat similar to that of SUPERHOT VR, where every action you take is crucial. So that players will not give up on this game by dying all the time, the death experience is made less sadistic by the fact that when the player unfortunately dies, they immediately respawn at the initial location of each archive node. I experienced the pain of dying in front of a storage point and having to replay it!

Each session is like a puzzle in a platformer, where the player must manoeuvre with precision while avoiding incoming enemy fire. It's not easy to do this, but the good thing is that each death allows the player to learn from experience and adjust their strategy to try again. While killing enemies feels great, it can get annoying to keep repeating the same levels over and over again!

USEC

In PMC mode, you will take on the role of a main character employed by USEC or BEAR. You will be born in the chosen map immediately at the start of the battle and carry the items and equipment you have equipped for the main character in your hideout. As a PMC player, you can kill other PMC players or Scav scavengers. To survive and evacuate from the battlefield with all your items, you must find and enter an available evacuation point. Double click on the O to see the evacuation point.

You can use the equipment in the hideout.

You can kill PMC and Scav.

You must evacuate to keep the items you have gained during the battle.

If you die or exceed the evacuation time, you will lose all items brought into the battle (except melee weapons and items in the safe)

Experience gained in the battle will count towards the main character's level experience

Quests can be completed

Scav

In Scav mode, you will play as a member of the Scav camp. You will be given random equipment (usually low level or unbalanced) and will be born halfway through the game (and without a message). Nevertheless, Scav mode has its advantages; the AI Scavs will not attack you unless you make the first move, and sometimes they will even go along with your actions. On top of this, Scav mode is free, which means you don't have to risk losing items as you do when playing as the PMC. However, Scav must also find and enter an extraction point, just like the PMC, in order to withdraw from the battle and transfer the loot. However, please note that the experience gained while playing as Scav does not go into the main PMC character experience; any experience gained in the battle will be lost and you can only complete the 'find items' category.

You will be given random equipment and it will be different each time.

You will be born halfway through the game with only limited information.

You can kill the PMC and Scav, but the Scav will not actively attack you.

You will lose all items equipped to your Scav character upon death or after the evacuation time.

Cannot complete any quests other than finding items.
